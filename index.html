Performance

Chinese Zither — Guzheng

Classical & Contemporary Repertoire

The guzheng is a 21-string plucked instrument with over 2,500 years of history. I've studied it for several years, performing both classical Chinese repertoire and contemporary arrangements. The instrument's expressive range — from delicate tremolo to sweeping glissandos — has shaped how I think about pacing, texture, and emotion in all creative work.

About

I enjoy exploring ideas through art, design, science, and technology. My interests range from visual storytelling and digital illustration to interaction design and biology-inspired experimentation.

I'm fascinated by how people experience the world — through stories, music, learning, and creativity — and I enjoy building projects that explore those experiences in new ways.

Outside of design and science, I study the guzheng, perform in musical theater, and enjoy experimenting with different artistic mediums including watercolor, ceramics, and digital painting.

I'm especially curious about how neuroscience, creative technology, and good design can intersect to build tools that genuinely help people think, feel, and connect.
