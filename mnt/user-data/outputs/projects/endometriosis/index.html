Endometriosis
Awareness

Raising awareness about a severely underdiagnosed condition affecting teen girls — exploring delayed diagnosis, menstrual pain, and the mental and social impacts through visual storytelling.

View on GitHub

Why It Matters

Average diagnosis takes 7–10 years.

Endometriosis affects roughly 1 in 10 people with a uterus — yet it remains massively underdiagnosed, particularly in teenagers. Symptoms are frequently dismissed as "normal period pain."

This project uses visual design and storytelling to make the condition visible, explain the diagnostic gap, and reduce stigma for teen girls navigating these experiences.

Delayed Diagnosis

On average, 7–10 years pass between first symptoms and a confirmed diagnosis.

Mental Health Impact

Chronic pain and dismissal contribute significantly to anxiety and depression in adolescents.

Social Impact

Missed school, social isolation, and academic disruption disproportionately affect teen girls.

Design Approach

Visibility through design.

This project combines visual storytelling, accessible information design, and teen-centered voice to make a complex medical topic approachable and shareable.

Infographic storytelling

Data and experience told through illustration and clear visual hierarchy.

Teen-centered voice

Language and framing that speaks directly to the audience experiencing it.

Reducing stigma

Normalizing the conversation around menstrual pain and chronic conditions.
