LOST
IN TIME

A story about Kai, a history nerd, three dismissive travel apps,
and the pair of smart glasses that almost changed everything.

Kai / age 16 / curious traveller

Character design & concept — Kai / angela1-creates  ·  10-panel storyboard

📚 History Books detailed but dry
→
📱 Travel Apps pictures + reviews
→
🎤 Tour Guides rigid and passive
→
But travelers need:
stories that appear naturally
during exploration.

✦   The Story   ✦

1
Senso-ji...
finally here! 😤
1 — Arrival
Tokyo. Senso-ji Temple.

Kai arrives at the ancient Kaminarimon gate — phone in hand, headphones around his neck, months of textbook reading behind him.

2
2 — The Blank
Wait… when was this built again?

He read an entire chapter. Annotated it. Highlighted it. And now? Standing right in front of Senso-ji — total white noise.

3
3 — Just Photos?!
"Liked by 2.4k people." Sure.

Every app. Same thing. Photos, star ratings, tourist reviews. Nothing about the 628 A.D. legend or the two brothers who found a golden goddess.

4
4 — The Spark
What if history found YOU?

A single thought hits Kai like a Tokyo train. Not an app. Not a tour guide. Smart glasses — lightweight, passive, woven into the walk itself.

5
5 — The Sketch
Scribbling before the idea escapes.

Kai's sketchbook comes out. "StoryLens" — smart glasses that detect your GPS, pull a 60-second story, and beam it right to your lens.

6
6 — StoryLens Prototype
See the place. Hear the story. At the same time.

The StoryLens concept: lightweight smart glasses with a HUD overlay. GPS pinpoints the temple, a 60-second story chapter plays through bone-conduction audio. No phone. No app. Just walk.

7
Okay so — what if your glasses just… told you the story?
Wait, like while you're walking?? 🤯
But do people actually want to learn history on vacation?
7 — Sharing the Idea
Friends. Coffee. Reality check.

Back home, Kai shows his sketchbook to friends. One is immediately hooked. The other raises the hardest question of all.

8
…honestly? I'd use this every time I travel.
Okay fine — make it optional. Not everyone has to wear the glasses.
8 — Real Feedback
"Make it optional. Not everyone wants the glasses."

The sceptic comes around — and gives Kai his first real design principle: optionality. Not everyone learns the same way. The curious traveller is the core user.

9
9 — The Insight
Not information. The feeling of being there.

Kai watches the sun sink behind Senso-ji. Good design isn't about how much you know. It's about whether you feel present in the story.

10
10 — StoryLens: The Vision
Smart glasses. Stories on demand. No phone required.

Kai stands at Senso-ji one last time — this time wearing StoryLens. Temple silhouette. Stars above. Chapters waiting. History is everywhere. You just need the right glasses to hear it.
