The Problem

Tools exist. Stories don't.

Travel apps show photos and reviews. History books are detailed but dry. Tour guides are rigid. None of them deliver stories that feel natural and alive in the moment of exploration.

The result: you stand in front of Senso-ji Temple — one of the world's most historically rich sites — and feel nothing beyond a vague sense that it's old and photogenic.

📚 History Books

Detailed but dry. Hard to connect with while you're physically at a place.

📱 Travel Apps

Photos and star ratings. No stories. No context. No why.

🎤 Tour Guides

Rigid schedules. Passive listening. No exploration.

The Direction

Stories that find you.

What if your location triggered a short, curated story — delivered through lightweight smart glasses, without needing to open an app or break your stride?

GPS-triggered stories

Arrive at a landmark. A 60-second story begins automatically.

Smart glasses HUD

Lightweight, reader-style frames. Story overlay on the lens. Bone-conduction audio.

Optional & passive

No phone out. No scrolling. Just walk. The story comes to you.

The Narrative

Follow Kai's journey.

The full story is told as an original 10-panel comic — following Kai, a curious 16-year-old traveler, from frustration at Senso-ji to sketching the StoryLens concept.
